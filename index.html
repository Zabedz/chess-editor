About the Chess Position Editor

The Chess Position Editor is a free online tool for setting up any chess position and reading the engine's verdict. Drag pieces onto the board, or paste a FEN or PGN to load a game, then pick whose turn it is. Stockfish 18 evaluates the position in your browser and shows the best move, the score from White's point of view, and the search depth as you edit. Nothing is installed and no account is needed.

How to use it

  1. Drag pieces from the palette onto the board, or paste a FEN or PGN into the load box to set a position.
  2. Choose White or Black to move with the turn toggle in the header.
  3. Read Stockfish's evaluation: the score bar, the numeric score, and the best move in algebraic and UCI notation.
  4. Press Forward to play the suggested move on the board, or Back to undo your last edit.

Frequently asked questions

What is a chess position editor?

It is a tool for building a single chess position piece by piece. Use it to study an endgame, set up a puzzle, or check a spot from one of your own games.

Is it free to use?

Yes. The editor and the Stockfish engine both run in your browser at no cost, with no sign-up.

Which chess engine does it use?

Stockfish 18, the open-source engine, compiled to JavaScript and run as a Web Worker so the analysis happens on your own device.

Can I analyze a position from one of my games?

Paste the FEN or PGN into the load box and the board updates to that position. The engine then evaluates from there.

Does it work on phones and tablets?

Yes. The layout adapts to smaller screens, and piece editing uses touch drag and drop.

Is my position sent to a server?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser, so your positions stay on your device.

Analysis runs on the open-source Stockfish engine and chess.js. View the source on GitHub.
